# About Aman Bhaskar

I'm an AI engineer obsessed with building systems that think, learn, and self-improve. My work sits at the intersection of distributed systems, agent orchestration, and production ML — the kind of engineering where you're not just building features, you're building *intelligence layers*.

## What I Do
I architect cognitive platforms — systems where multiple AI models collaborate through structured graphs, retrieving from hybrid knowledge bases, maintaining multi-tier memory, and streaming responses in real-time. Think: LangGraph orchestration, not LangChain chains.

## How I Got Here
Started as a full-stack engineer building traditional web apps. The moment I deployed my first RAG pipeline and watched it synthesize answers from a knowledge base I'd built, I was hooked. Moved deeper into agent architectures, distributed inference, and now I'm building systems where the AI doesn't just answer — it understands context, remembers conversations across sessions, and adapts to who's asking.

## What Makes Me Different
- I build the full stack — from PostgreSQL schemas to Three.js particle systems
- I optimize for production, not demos — every system I build handles graceful degradation
- I think in graphs, not chains — my architectures are node-based with conditional routing
- I ship fast but harden relentlessly — observable, testable, self-healing by default

## Current Obsessions
- Multi-model orchestration (routing different queries to different LLMs)
- Self-healing knowledge bases (semantic drift detection, freshness scoring)  
- Visitor intelligence (adapting AI responses based on who's asking)
- Memory architectures that span sessions (Redis → PostgreSQL → Qdrant)

## Beyond Code
I read about cognitive science, complex systems theory, and how the brain does memory consolidation. These ideas directly influence how I design AI memory systems. I believe the best AI systems are inspired by cognitive architecture, not just scaling laws.
